SaaS Metrics

Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) companies are fundamentally driven by metrics. Unlike traditional businesses that may rely primarily on revenue and profit, SaaS companies must understand customer behavior, retention, growth efficiency, unit economics, and expansion dynamics.

The purpose of SaaS metrics is not reporting.

The purpose is decision making.

Good metrics reveal:

Growth quality
Customer value
Business health
Operational efficiency
Future potential

The Fundamental Principle

Revenue alone can be misleading.

Example:

A startup may double revenue while losing customers rapidly.

Another startup may grow slowly but retain customers exceptionally well.

The second startup may ultimately become more valuable.

Therefore SaaS companies must analyze systems rather than isolated numbers.

Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

Definition

Predictable monthly revenue generated from subscriptions.

Formula

Sum of all monthly subscription revenue.

Example:

100 customers × $100/month

MRR = $10,000

Importance

MRR is the primary growth metric for most SaaS companies.

Benefits:

Predictability
Comparability
Planning

MRR Categories

New MRR

Revenue from new customers.

Expansion MRR

Revenue from existing customers upgrading.

Contraction MRR

Revenue lost through downgrades.

Churned MRR

Revenue lost from cancellations.

Healthy SaaS companies maximize:

New MRR
Expansion MRR

while minimizing:

Churned MRR
Contraction MRR

Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)

Definition

Yearly recurring subscription revenue.

Formula

ARR = MRR × 12

Example:

$50,000 MRR

ARR = $600,000

ARR is commonly used by investors and later-stage SaaS companies.

Revenue Growth Rate

Measures business growth.

Formula

(Current Revenue − Previous Revenue)

÷

Previous Revenue

× 100

Importance

Growth indicates market demand.

Benchmarks

Early Stage:

10-20% monthly growth

Strong Startup:

20%+ monthly growth

Later Stage:

Lower growth but greater revenue scale

Growth should be sustainable.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Definition

Cost required to acquire one customer.

Formula

Sales + Marketing Expenses

÷

New Customers

Example:

$20,000 spent.

100 customers acquired.

CAC = $200

Importance

CAC measures acquisition efficiency.

Lower CAC improves profitability.

Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

Definition

Expected revenue generated by a customer during the relationship.

Simplified Formula

Average Revenue Per Account

×

Customer Lifetime

Example:

$100/month

36 months

LTV = $3,600

Importance

LTV estimates customer value.

LTV:CAC Ratio

One of the most important SaaS metrics.

Formula

LTV ÷ CAC

Example:

LTV = $3,600

CAC = $300

Ratio = 12

Benchmarks

Below 1

Unsustainable.

1-3

Needs improvement.

3-5

Healthy.

Above 5

Strong economics.

Excessively high ratios may indicate underinvestment in growth.

CAC Payback Period

Definition

Time required to recover acquisition cost.

Formula

CAC

÷

Monthly Gross Profit

Example:

CAC = $600

Monthly profit = $100

Payback = 6 months

Importance

Shorter payback improves cash flow.

Benchmarks

Excellent:

Less than 6 months

Good:

6-12 months

Risky:

More than 18 months

Customer Churn Rate

Definition

Percentage of customers lost.

Formula

Lost Customers

÷

Total Customers

× 100

Example:

10 customers lost.

100 customers total.

Churn = 10%

Importance

Churn destroys growth.

A startup can acquire thousands of customers.

If retention is weak, growth collapses.

Revenue Churn

Measures lost recurring revenue.

Formula

Lost MRR

÷

Total MRR

× 100

Revenue churn often matters more than customer churn.

Losing large customers can significantly impact growth.

Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

One of the most important SaaS metrics.

Definition

Revenue retained after accounting for:

Churn
Downgrades
Expansions

Formula

Starting MRR

Expansion

−

Contraction

−

Churn

÷

Starting MRR

× 100

Benchmarks

Below 100%

Revenue shrinking.

100%

Stable.

110-120%

Strong.

120%+

Exceptional.

High NRR indicates expansion revenue exceeds losses.

Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)

Definition

Revenue retained excluding expansion revenue.

Measures customer loyalty directly.

Benchmarks

90%+ generally considered strong.

Activation Rate

Definition

Percentage of users reaching first meaningful value.

Formula

Activated Users

÷

Total Users

× 100

Examples of Activation

First report created
First transaction completed
First project launched

Activation strongly influences retention.

Retention Rate

Definition

Percentage of users continuing usage.

Formula

Retained Users

÷

Original Users

× 100

Retention is often the clearest Product-Market Fit signal.

Investors care deeply about retention.

Daily Active Users (DAU)

Users active daily.

Useful for:

Consumer SaaS
Collaboration tools
Productivity platforms

Monthly Active Users (MAU)

Users active monthly.

Measures overall engagement.

DAU/MAU Ratio

Definition

Engagement frequency.

Formula

DAU ÷ MAU

Benchmarks

20%

Moderate.

40%

Strong.

60%+

Very strong.

High ratios indicate habit formation.

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)

Definition

Average revenue generated per customer.

Formula

Total Revenue

÷

Number of Customers

Importance

Tracks monetization efficiency.

Increasing ARPU often improves profitability.

Expansion Revenue

Definition

Additional revenue generated from existing customers.

Examples:

Upgrades
Additional seats
New modules

Importance

Expansion is often cheaper than acquisition.

Best SaaS businesses grow revenue from existing customers.

Burn Rate

Definition

Monthly cash consumption.

Formula

Cash Outflows − Cash Inflows

Importance

Determines financial survival.

Runway

Definition

Months remaining before cash depletion.

Formula

Cash Balance

÷

Monthly Burn

Example:

$120,000 cash.

$10,000 burn.

Runway = 12 months.

Founders should monitor runway continuously.

Magic Number

Measures sales efficiency.

Formula

Quarterly ARR Increase × 4

÷

Sales & Marketing Spend

Benchmarks

Below 0.5

Weak.

0.75+

Healthy.

1+

Strong.

Rule of 40

Popular SaaS benchmark.

Formula

Growth Rate + Profit Margin

Example:

Growth = 30%

Profit = 15%

Rule of 40 = 45

Benchmark

40+ generally indicates healthy balance between growth and profitability.

North Star Metric

Definition

Single metric most closely representing customer value.

Examples

Collaboration Platform

Projects completed.

Learning Platform

Lessons completed.

Marketplace

Transactions completed.

A strong North Star aligns customer success with business success.

The Ultimate SaaS Metric

Many founders believe revenue is the most important metric.

In reality, the most important metric is often retention.

Why?

Because retention proves value.

Strong retention leads to:

Revenue growth
Referrals
Expansion
Lower CAC
Better economics

Without retention, growth becomes expensive and temporary.

With retention, growth compounds naturally.

The Ultimate Goal

SaaS metrics are not merely numbers.

They are signals describing customer value, business quality, and future potential.

The best SaaS companies do not optimize metrics individually.

They create products that generate genuine customer value.

When value is consistently delivered:

Retention improves
Churn declines
Revenue grows
Expansion increases
Economics strengthen

and the metrics naturally reflect the health of the business.
